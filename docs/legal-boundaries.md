# Legal Boundaries

Nevins Port is receive-only by design. It has no transmit path, no jamming path, no spoofing path, and no feature that presents RF activity as evidence of a specific person, home, vehicle, or private device owner.

## Conservative Classification

Unknown signals are treated conservatively. When a signal could plausibly be restricted/private, Nevins Port routes it to explanation and warning rather than content decoding.

## No Private-Content Decoding

The system may analyze signal shape, timing, bandwidth, modulation class, frequency range, and public metadata. It does not decrypt protected communications or extract private message content.

## No Cellular Interception

Nevins Port does not include cellular interception features and does not position itself as a tool for monitoring cellular communications.

## No Decryption

Protected or private communications are not decrypted. The product boundary is receive-only RF interpretation, capture, replay, and explanation for lawful public and educational use.

## No Transmit Functionality

There is no transmission support. The RTL-SDR hardware path is receive-only, and the software does not provide jamming, spoofing, or signal injection controls.

## No Targeting Individuals

Signal cards must not claim that an RF observation identifies a private person, device owner, home, or vehicle. A card may say "likely OOK/ASK burst" or "unknown narrowband signal"; it must not invent a private identity.

## Lawful Educational/Public RF Focus

Supported examples include broadcast FM, weather radio where available, amateur radio, lawful public aviation signals, satellite weather imagery where lawful, ISM-band device-class analysis, and replayable IQ research.
